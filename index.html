About

I help organisations adopt AI into their workflows and behaviours — ensuring they are fully ready and keeping pace with the latest technology shifts. With agentic AI, the hard-won knowledge of domain experts can now be packaged into small, accessible tools that the wider organisation can use. What once took a remote sensing specialist weeks to set up — processing Sentinel-1 SAR imagery to detect flood inundations — can today be vibe-coded into a working tool that anyone on the team can run, without losing the rigour behind it. Vibe coding into the regular workplace is the future, and I work to make that transition real for organisations.

At the UN Resident Coordinator's Office in Nepal, I put this into practice — building real-time analytics engines, bridging UNINFO and UN agencies through browser automations, and rethinking how knowledge reaches people. Tools like ElevenLabs and Seedance open a new opportunity to move beyond traditional slide decks: we are experimenting with characters, animations, and music to change how content is delivered. I bring Claude, Copilot, and Design Intelligence together in practical daily workflows.

Earlier roles span engineering at Logpoint, field operations at UNICEF, IoMT at Kipuwex, and cloud evangelism across Nepal — cross-functional teams, 5+ countries, analytics at scale. On the code side: Python and JavaScript for pipelines, automation, and APIs. Arctic Code Vault Contributor. Open-source contributions merged into Canonical (e.g. charm-eng-releases) and WordPress repositories.

Reach out if you're working on AI adoption, humanitarian technology, or making complex tools accessible to everyone.
